# Class 10 Science Textbook Content

## Section: Chemical Reactions
A chemical reaction is a process that leads to the chemical transformation of one set of chemical substances to another. Classically, chemical reactions encompass changes that only involve the positions of electrons in the forming and breaking of chemical bonds between atoms.
Chemical reactions can be balanced by ensuring that the number of atoms of each element remains the same on both the reactant and product sides of the equation.
Key reaction types include combination reactions, decomposition reactions, displacement, and redox (reduction-oxidation) where transfer of electrons occurs.
Keywords: chemical reaction, balancing equations, redox, combination, decomposition, displacement.

## Section: Acids, Bases, and Salts
Acids are substances that produce hydrogen ions (H+) in aqueous solutions. Bases are substances that produce hydroxide ions (OH-) in aqueous solutions.
The pH scale measures how acidic or basic a water-soluble substance is. It ranges from 0 to 14. A pH of 7 is neutral. pH values below 7 indicate acidity, whereas pH values above 7 indicate basicity.
Salts are ionic compounds formed from the neutralization reaction of an acid and a base. Common salts include sodium chloride (NaCl), bleaching powder (CaOCl2), baking soda (NaHCO3), and washing soda (Na2CO3).
Keywords: pH scale, acid, base, salt, neutralization, baking soda, bleaching powder, sodium chloride.

## Section: Metals and Non-Metals
Metals are elements that conduct heat and electricity, are malleable and ductile. They form basic oxides when combined with oxygen. Gold is highly ductile.
Non-metals are generally poor conductors of heat and electricity. They form acidic or neutral oxides.
The reactivity series arranges metals in decreasing order of their reactivity. Metals high in the series displace lower metals from their salt solutions.
Corrosion is the gradual destruction of metals by chemical reaction with their environment, such as rust on iron. It can be prevented by galvanizing, painting, or oiling.
Keywords: metals, ductility, reactivity series, corrosion, galvanizing, non-metals.

## Section: Carbon and its Compounds
Carbon has a valency of four (tetravalent) and can form covalent bonds with other carbon atoms (catenation). This enables carbon to form millions of organic compounds.
Saturated carbon compounds (alkanes) contain single covalent bonds. Unsaturated carbon compounds (alkenes and alkynes) contain double or triple bonds.
Homologous series is a group of organic compounds having the same functional group and similar chemical properties, differing by a -CH2- unit.
Soaps are sodium or potassium salts of long-chain carboxylic acids. Detergents are ammonium or sulfonate salts.
Keywords: covalent bond, carbon compounds, alkanes, alkenes, homologous series, soaps, detergents.

## Section: Life Processes
Life processes are the basic vital functions performed by living organisms to sustain their life on Earth. These include nutrition, respiration, transportation, and excretion.
Nutrition involves intake of food. Plants perform autotrophic nutrition via photosynthesis: 6CO2 + 6H2O + Sunlight -> C6H12O6 + 6O2. Respiration releases energy from food.
Transportation in plants occurs via Xylem (water and minerals) and Phloem (food). In humans, the heart pumps blood through arteries and veins.
Excretion is the removal of nitrogenous waste from the body, performed by kidneys and nephrons.
Keywords: photosynthesis, xylem, phloem, respiration, nutrition, excretion, kidney.
